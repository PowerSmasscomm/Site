Solar Energy

It is the energy that is derived from the sun, and this solar energy is great, as the energy that reaches the earth in one hour is greater than the energy consumed by the world in a year, and solar energy is produced from the sun using photovoltaic solar panels, as these panels contain cells that absorb heat and convert it into energy consumable. It is characterized by being reliable as it depends on the sun, as it does not pollute the environment, and it produces enough electricity for the place in which it is installed, and sometimes more.

Why solar energy?

Because Egypt is one of the countries in the Sunbelt region most suitable for solar energy applications, there is no denying the importance of solar energy, because the return on investment from using solar energy is of high value, not only in financial terms, but also for public health and environmental sustainability. And with each passing year, solar energy becomes the perfect solution for owners of projects, farms, companies and homes. If you are interested in the importance of solar energy, we have more than a few reasons why solar energy is the perfect alternative.

  • Solar energy is good for the environment.
  • Solar energy can improve energy security
  • It can reduce your electricity bills
  • Save you money in the long time .

The Benban solar complex adds 2,000 megawatts of electricity to the national grid

Solar panels are usually made of several units of photovoltaic cells made of semiconductor materials such as silicon that form an electrical circuit, and when the panel traps sunlight, electrons are emitted and are saved as an electric current

The Benban complex includes that each company or investor has one or two plots of land on which photovoltaic cells are built, and all of them are connected to stations that, in turn, connect the electricity produced to the unified network. The project site was chosen based on studies and reports NASA and some international scientific institutions, which confirmed the comparative advantage of Aswan in establishing these projects in the field of solar energy. The Benban solar power plant comes as one of the sources of energy diversification and maximization of the use of Renewable energy. The project's electricity production is equivalent to 90% of the energy produced from the High Dam.

Solar water pumping systems

Solar water pumping systems are used for irrigation and drinking water in India in the vast majority of small farms that need pumps of less than 3000 watts. The Egyptian market has shown a great demand for this economic system, which does not need any storage batteries. Solar pumps represent more than 80% of the volume of business in stores that are operated by the private sector in Egypt In the practical application of solar energy, defects appeared in some projects implemented in Egypt as a result of non-observance of the principles of proper design and implementation, which leads to an insufficient amount of water pumped for agricultural needs.

Off-grid solar PV

• Stand-alone systems for remote and off-grid homes. • Solar street lighting.

The energy generated by the sun is considered variable because it depends on the weather, and this change is considered a challenge for the use of solar energy for homes, where the amount of energy produced must be balanced with the demand on the grid, as the success of the solar energy system depends on the availability of high solar radiation on the site and in the absence of a storage system, it It is necessary to work on developing weather forecasting skills, which gives semi-accurate results about the amount of energy produced, and the best solution is to use an economical and efficient storage system, where excess energy is stored when the solar radiation is high, to be used later at the time when it is declining. It has the rate of solar radiation during the night or the winter season, for example, and since the domestic consumption of energy is rather low, a medium-efficiency storage system can be adopted to meet household needs without causing environmental pollution.

Egypt's efforts

Egypt2030

Egypt's 2030 vision in energy

• The Egyptian government realizes the need for a sustainable and diversified mix of energy to meet the needs of the youth and the growing population, and therefore renewable energy is an indispensable element to achieve the development goals of Egypt's Vision 2030. • Based on Egypt's Vision 2030, the Ministry of Electricity and Renewable Energy issued in 2015 its sustainable energy strategy until 2035, which aims to ensure continuous and diversified energy security through cross-sector cooperation in renewable energy sources. • Egypt intends to increase the supply of electricity generated from renewable sources to 20% by 2022 and 42% by 2035, while providing wind energy by 14%, hydropower by 2%, and solar energy by 25% of energy.

Transition to renewable energy

• The Egyptian government began its transition towards energy security by increasing renewable energy in 2014, when it entered into a partnership with the World Bank to carry out reforms in the energy sector and attract $2 billion in investments in renewable energy sources. • The Egyptian government has concluded several major deals with international banks to finance projects such as the Benban Solar Energy Complex, which will be the largest solar energy project in the world once completed. • The government has received more than $650 million in financing from the International Finance Corporation (IFC), a member of the World Bank Group, to build 13 solar power plants that form part of the Benban Solar Park project

The objectives of Egypt's Vision 2030 in the field of energy.

• Targeted power generation on the basis of renewable energy. • Reducing carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions in order to develop a low carbon society • The need to diversify from natural gas mostly to a mixture of renewable and conventional energy sources in order to improve energy security and reduce dependence on fossil fuels. • With a plan to become a regional energy center, Egypt is building wind farms and solar power plants and increasing its hydroelectric capacity, with the aim of increasing the share of renewable energy to 43% of energy consumption by 2035

The most prominent solar energy projects in Egypt

Benban, the world's solar energy capital.

The project is located in the village of Benban, 35 km north of Aswan, on an area of ​​37 km². The most important and largest electrical projects that have been implemented in the world in recent years. - The largest solar power plant in the world, with a capacity of 1465 megawatts. - The largest gathering of solar energy plants with a voltaic cell system without storage in the world. The project aims to mobilize private investments to build the largest photovoltaic generation plants in the world. - Work on the project began in 2015 in accordance with Republican Decree No. 274 of 2014. The site includes 40 solar plants to generate electricity The number of solar panels used in the station is about 200,000 solar panels. The proportion of capacities generated from renewable energies on the national electricity grid is 20% by 2022. Each station has a capacity of 50 megawatts.

The most prominent solar energy projects in Egypt

Zafarana station in Suez

The Egyptian New and Renewable Energy Authority and the Belectric-CCC consortium have signed a contract to install, operate and maintain an electricity production plant using photovoltaic solar systems with a capacity of 50 megawatts in the city of Zafarana, Suez Governorate. - The project is the first project to generate electricity using solar cells to be implemented in the Zafarana area in Suez Governorate, which opens the door for more investments in the field of solar energy in this region. The project is being implemented on an area of ​​about one square kilometer, and the project is expected to produce about 90,000 megawatt-hours annually, saving about 19,000 tons of oil equivalent and reducing emissions of about 50 thousand tons of carbon dioxide. The project provides about 500 temporary job opportunities during the preparation and construction period, as well as about 50 permanent jobs during operation and maintenance throughout the project’s 25-year lifespan.

The most prominent solar energy projects in Egypt

The Kom Ombo power plant is the largest solar power plant in Egypt

The new Kom Ombo plant is located less than 20 kilometers from the largest solar energy complex in Africa, the 1.8 GW Benban complex. Once operational, the new utility-wide plant will serve 130,000 families. ACWA Power submitted the lowest tariff in the first solar PV tender in Egypt. The provision of solar energy through a public tender process aims to achieve a competitive tariff and promote the growth of solar energy as an affordable alternative to traditional energy sources. - The participation of the private sector in the Kom Ombo project is the result of a successful policy dialogue with the Ministry of Electricity and Renewable Energy and the Egyptian Electricity Transmission Company, in addition to a technical assistance program worth US$ 3.6 million, co-financed by the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development and the Green Climate Fund, to support the Egyptian Company For electricity transmission in the management of competitive tenders for renewable energy.
